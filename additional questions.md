U.S. Fiscal Policy Quiz (20 MCQs)

1. Fiscal policy in the United States primarily refers to:
A. The Federal Reserve’s adjustment of interest rates.
B. Government decisions on spending and taxation to influence the economy.
C. Rules for regulating financial markets.
D. Policies to stabilize the value of the U.S. dollar.

2. Which institution has the constitutional authority to raise revenue and authorize federal spending?
A. The Federal Reserve
B. The U.S. Congress
C. The Supreme Court
D. The Department of Commerce

3. Which of the following is an example of expansionary fiscal policy?
A. Raising the federal funds rate
B. Cutting personal income tax rates
C. Selling Treasury securities to banks
D. Reducing unemployment insurance eligibility

4. A contractionary fiscal policy designed to help cool inflation would most likely:
A. Increase infrastructure spending
B. Reduce government spending or raise taxes
C. Increase unemployment benefits
D. Lower corporate tax rates

5. A federal budget deficit occurs when:
A. Federal revenues exceed federal outlays.
B. Federal outlays exceed federal revenues.
C. Imports exceed exports.
D. State and local taxes are higher than federal taxes.

6. Which office provides Congress with nonpartisan projections about the federal budget and the economy?
A. Office of Management and Budget (OMB)
B. Congressional Budget Office (CBO)
C. Council of Economic Advisers (CEA)
D. Government Accountability Office (GAO)

7. Automatic stabilizers in U.S. fiscal policy are best described as:
A. One-time emergency stimulus bills passed by Congress.
B. Budget mechanisms that automatically increase spending or lower taxes when the economy weakens.
C. Monetary tools used by the Federal Reserve.
D. Tariff schedules that change with trade flows.

8. Which of the following is a clear example of an automatic stabilizer?
A. The defense budget
B. The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)
C. A temporary tax holiday passed by Congress
D. A corporate subsidy for exports

9. Discretionary federal spending is:
A. Spending that is determined each year through the congressional appropriations process.
B. Spending that is permanently authorized, such as Social Security.
C. Interest payments on the national debt.
D. State-level education spending.

10. The first formal step in the federal budget process each year is that:
A. The Supreme Court approves agency budgets.
B. The President submits a budget request to Congress.
C. The Federal Reserve publishes its monetary policy report.
D. The CBO issues a continuing resolution.

11. If unemployment rises sharply, which fiscal action would support aggregate demand the fastest?
A. Reducing eligibility for SNAP
B. Increasing funding for unemployment insurance
C. Raising payroll taxes
D. Cutting federal hiring across agencies

12. Which statement correctly distinguishes fiscal policy from monetary policy?
A. Both are controlled entirely by the Federal Reserve.
B. Fiscal policy uses taxes and spending; monetary policy uses interest rates and the money supply.
C. Fiscal policy cannot address recessions.
D. Monetary policy is written by Congress in annual appropriations bills.

13. Why do persistent large federal deficits create long-run policy concerns?
A. They automatically eliminate unemployment.
B. They can push federal debt higher, raising interest costs and reducing future fiscal flexibility.
C. They prevent the Federal Reserve from lowering rates.
D. They immediately stop all automatic stabilizers.

14. Large, one-time stimulus measures that Congress passes during recessions are examples of:
A. Automatic stabilizers
B. Discretionary fiscal policy
C. Monetary tightening
D. Regulatory reform

15. To fund most federal agencies each year, Congress must pass:
A. Appropriations bills
B. Constitutional amendments
C. Supreme Court writs
D. International treaties

16. In fiscal policy, transfer payments (like unemployment benefits) can be thought of as:
A. Negative taxes that increase households’ disposable income.
B. Tools used only by state governments.
C. Purely capital expenditures on infrastructure.
D. Monetary policy instruments.

17. Which combination below represents contractionary fiscal policy?
A. Lower taxes and higher spending
B. Higher taxes and lower spending
C. Higher taxes and higher spending
D. Lower taxes and lower spending

18. Which of the following best describes the President’s budget?
A. It is legally binding on Congress.
B. It is a policy proposal that signals priorities; Congress must enact actual tax and spending laws.
C. It is written by the Federal Reserve.
D. It cannot include tax changes.

19. Automatic stabilizers help the economy during a downturn because they:
A. Require new legislation every time the economy slows.
B. Activate quickly as incomes fall, supporting household spending.
C. Always reduce the budget deficit.
D. Are funded only by state governments.

20. Which organization issues a regular “Monthly Budget Review” that tracks U.S. deficits?
A. Federal Reserve Board
B. Congressional Budget Office (CBO)
C. Department of Energy
D. World Bank

Answer Key and Explanations
1. Answer: B – Fiscal policy is the use of government spending and taxation to influence economic conditions.
2. Answer: B – Congress holds the power of the purse and passes tax and spending laws.
3. Answer: B – Tax cuts increase disposable income and support aggregate demand.
4. Answer: B – Contractionary policy aims to reduce demand by cutting spending or increasing taxes.
5. Answer: B – A deficit means the government spends more than it takes in during a fiscal year.
6. Answer: B – The CBO is Congress’s nonpartisan budget analyst.
7. Answer: B – Automatic stabilizers like unemployment insurance respond without new legislation.
8. Answer: B – SNAP spending rises automatically when more households qualify in downturns.
9. Answer: A – Discretionary spending is set annually in appropriations bills.
10. Answer: B – By law, the President sends Congress a budget proposal early in the year.
11. Answer: B – Boosting unemployment insurance quickly puts money in the hands of jobless workers.
12. Answer: B – Fiscal policy is legislative/executive; monetary policy is central bank–driven.
13. Answer: B – Higher debt means more resources go to interest, limiting room for future policy.
14. Answer: B – They require specific legislation and are not automatic.
15. Answer: A – Annual appropriations provide budget authority for many programs.
16. Answer: A – Transfers raise spending power similarly to a tax cut.
17. Answer: B – Higher taxes plus lower spending both reduce aggregate demand.
18. Answer: B – Congressional action is required before spending/revenue changes take effect.
19. Answer: B – They are countercyclical by design and do not require congressional delay.
20. Answer: B – The CBO publishes the Monthly Budget Review that reports on the federal budget.
